Our Sustainable Farm Partners

Moon Valley Farms (CA)

Organic heirloom produce from California's coastal valleys. Moon Valley Farms cultivates heirloom fruits and vegetables using organic, non-GMO methods that preserve seed diversity and enrich the soil.

Rise Bakery (Local)

Artisan sourdough with zero preservatives. A local favorite known for hand-crafted, naturally fermented breads baked daily with simple, whole ingredients.

Tropical Foods (Vietnam)

Fair trade nuts grown with care. Sourcing cashews from smallholder farms committed to ethical labor and organic practices in Vietnam's central highlands.

Earth's Harvest Farm (Oregon)

Regenerative mushroom cultivation in the Pacific Northwest. This Oregon-based farm grows mushrooms using closed-loop systems that restore soil vitality and reduce waste.

Gold Ridge Orchards (CA)

Locally grown walnuts with minimal impact. Family-owned and sustainably managed, Gold Ridge Orchards focuses on eco-conscious practices and water-efficient nut farming.

Suncrest Farms (OR)

Organic greens rooted in soil health. Specializing in vibrant vegetables grown through organic, no-till methods that protect local pollinators and water tables.

Lotus Foods (CA)

Biodiverse rice grown with less water. Partnering with global co-ops, Lotus Foods supports regenerative rice farming that reduces methane emissions and improves livelihoods.

Coastal Roots Farm (CA)

Faith-based organic farming for food justice. A nonprofit farm that blends organic agriculture with education and food access for underserved communities in Southern California.

Harmony Fields (WA)

Local produce with pollinator-friendly farming. This small farm in Washington cultivates kale, tomatoes, and herbs without synthetic inputs, focusing on ecological harmony.

Timeless Natural Foods (MT)

Organic pulses from the northern plains. Pioneers of organic lentils and chickpeas, this Montana-based co-op champions soil-building crop rotations and fair pricing.

Fruitful Harvest Co-op (CA)

Fair trade dried fruits from small orchards. This California cooperative sources fruit sustainably and processes it using solar-powered dryers and composting initiatives.

Red Barn Farm (WA)

Locally grown vegetables and community resilience. This farm uses integrated pest management and organic practices to produce crisp, flavorful veggies for the local community.

Runamok (VT)

Award-winning infused and pure maple syrups. Runamok uses sustainable tapping and solar-powered processing in Vermont's maple groves.

Bluebird Farms (WA)

Organic grains from the Pacific Northwest. Bluebird focuses on heritage grains grown with regenerative practices for soil health and biodiversity.

Drake's Family Farms (CA)

Pasture-raised dairy with a purpose. Drake's produces goat milk and cheese with humane animal care, rotational grazing, and zero synthetic hormones.

Foraged & Found (OR)

Wild foods from the Pacific forests. Foraged & Found ethically harvests wild greens, mushrooms, and berries with respect for indigenous knowledge and ecosystems.

Rainshadow Organics (OR)

A full-diet farm using closed-loop organic systems. Rainshadow grows grains, vegetables, and livestock on a diversified, sustainable model in Central Oregon.

Harvest Moon Farm (OR)

Family-run organic farm producing heirloom vegetables. Harvest Moon focuses on seasonal crops, low-waste packaging, and biodiversity conservation.

Smiling Hara Tempeh (NC)

Craft tempeh from sustainable soy and legumes. Smiling Hara blends local ingredients with ancient fermentation techniques to create nutritious, minimally processed tempeh.

Upton's Naturals (Sri Lanka)

Innovators in jackfruit and seitan. Upton's sources jackfruit from Sri Lankan farmers practicing organic and sustainable agriculture.

Navitas Organics (Peru)

Superfoods grown with purpose. Navitas partners with Peruvian farms growing organic cacao, maca, and chia, promoting soil health and fair trade.

The Chia Co. (Australia)

Sun-ripened chia from the outback. The Chia Co uses traceable supply chains and solar-powered facilities for their nutrient-dense chia products.

Organic Traditions (Italy)

Superfoods rooted in heritage. Sourcing herbs, fruits, and roots from European farmers using organic and regenerative techniques for nutrient-rich blends.

Native Forest (CA)

Organic canned goods with a conscience. Native Forest uses sustainable coconut, bamboo, and palm resources while protecting rainforests and indigenous livelihoods.

Numi Organic Tea (India)

Fair trade tea with a mission. Numi partners with tea gardens in India that use organic, biodynamic practices and invest in community development.

Oatly (Sweden)

Oat milk with a low carbon footprint. Oatly develops plant-based dairy alternatives with a focus on sustainability, transparency, and climate justice.

Alter Eco (Bolivia)

Regenerative quinoa and farmer equity. This B Corp partners with Bolivian co-ops to grow quinoa organically in ways that restore biodiversity and empower indigenous communities.

Frontier Co-op (Global)

Ethical spices from community growers worldwide. Frontier sources high-quality spices through direct trade with farmers committed to organic and fair labor standards.

Madhava Organic (Mexico)

Pure honey and agave from small-scale Mexican farms. Madhava partners with organic producers and tests for glyphosate-free purity in every batch.
